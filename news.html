SWIM-360: Multimodal Explainable AI for Holistic Swim Optimisation

Project brief

The SWIM-360 project aims to revolutionise competitive swimming performance analysis by integrating multimodal AI and sports science. Funded by Xjenza Malta's Thematic Programmes and the Malta Digital Innovation Authority (2024-2026), this research seeks to address critical limitations in current training methods. The primary innovation is the development of an AI system that combines real-time sensor data with video analysis to enable advanced biomechanical tracking and performance optimization. Key objectives include: (1)Developing a multimodal dataset that integrates synchronized video and sensor data. (2) Implementing real-time performance monitoring through wearable sensor integration; and (3) Applying machine learning algorithms for stroke optimization and injury risk prevention.

Methodology

The project utilises a comprehensive approach involving real-time data processing and advanced AI technologies. A central feature of the methodology is the use of an explainable AI framework, which ensures transparency in performance feedback and fosters better communication between coaches and athletes. The methodology includes collaboration with the Aquatic Sports Association of Malta for practical validation, targeting Technology Readiness Level 6. Key tools include wearable sensors for real-time monitoring and sophisticated video analysis software for biomechanical tracking, which together form a robust system for performance enhancement.

Expected Outcomes

The expected outcomes of the SWIM-360 project include significant advancements in academic knowledge concerning AI applications in sports science, as well as the development of innovative training methodologies for competitive swimming. The project also aims to create scalable frameworks for performance analysis that can be applied to other sports. Beyond theoretical advancements, the research has practical implications for improving athletic training, stroke optimisation, and injury prevention, contributing to the broader field of digital technologies in sports and aligning with Malta's AI strategy.
